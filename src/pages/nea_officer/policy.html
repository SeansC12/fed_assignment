NEA Operational Policies

This page outlines the official inspection, grading, and enforcement policies governing hawker stalls under the National Environment Agency. These guidelines are intended for internal reference by authorised officers.

Hygiene Grading Standards

Grade A

Excellent hygiene practices. Full compliance with food safety standards.

Grade B

Good hygiene standards with minor, non-critical lapses.

Grade C

Fair hygiene standards. Improvement required within stipulated time.

Grade D

Poor hygiene standards. Subject to enforcement and possible suspension.

Inspection Frequency

  • Grade A & B stalls: Routine inspections every 6–12 months
  • Grade C stalls: Follow-up inspection within 3 months
  • Grade D stalls: Immediate re-inspection and enforcement action
  • Complaint-based inspections may occur at any time

Public Complaint Handling

Complaints submitted by members of the public are reviewed and categorised based on severity and frequency.

  • Multiple complaints trigger priority inspection
  • Serious allegations require immediate site assessment
  • False or unverified reports are documented but monitored

Enforcement Actions

  • Written warnings and corrective action plans
  • Monetary penalties under NEA regulations
  • Temporary suspension of stall operations
  • Licence revocation for repeated non-compliance

Officer Responsibilities

  • Conduct inspections objectively and professionally
  • Ensure inspection records are accurate and timely
  • Document photographic evidence where applicable
  • Maintain confidentiality of internal records
